This discovery highlights a critical security vulnerability in voice AI systems, similar to adversarial attacks in computer vision, and underscores the need for robust defenses as voice AI becomes more integrated into daily life. Unlike previous attacks that required full control over the audio input and instructions, AudioHijack can embed hidden commands in regular audio clips, making it more stealthy and practical. The attack targets LALMs that accept audio instructions, a growing trend in generative AI.

hackernews · SVI · May 18, 11:51 · [Discussion](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=48178378)

**Background**: Adversarial attacks are well-known in image recognition, where small perturbations to an image can cause a model to misclassify it. Voice AI systems, such as speech recognition and voice assistants, are now facing similar threats. AudioHijack specifically targets large audio-language models (LALMs), which combine audio processing with language understanding, making them susceptible to hidden audio commands that are imperceptible to humans.
