## [Forge boosts 8B model accuracy from 53% to 99% with guardrails](https://github.com/antoinezambelli/forge) ⭐️ 8.0/10

Forge, an open-source reliability layer for self-hosted LLMs, improves an 8B model's accuracy on multi-step agentic tasks from ~53% to ~99% using domain-agnostic guardrails, without modifying the model itself. This demonstrates that local, consumer-hardware models can rival frontier APIs in reliability for agentic workflows, potentially reducing costs and increasing adoption of self-hosted AI systems. Forge's guardrail stack includes five layers: retry nudges, step enforcement, error recovery, rescue parsing, and context compaction; ablation studies show retry nudges and error recovery are most impactful. The system also introduces a ToolResolutionError exception to distinguish successful tool calls from empty results.

hackernews · zambelli · May 19, 12:23 · [Discussion](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=48192383)

**Background**: LLM guardrails are rules and filters that ensure safe, on-topic behavior from language models. For agentic tasks, where models call external tools in multi-step workflows, per-step accuracy compounds: 90% per step over 5 steps yields only ~59% overall success. Forge addresses this by adding structural reliability mechanisms around the model, independent of the model's capabilities.
