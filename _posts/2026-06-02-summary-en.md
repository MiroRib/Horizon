Intel announced plans to invest $3.3 billion to build a new factory in India that will produce advanced base substrates for its next-generation chips. This investment strengthens Intel's global manufacturing footprint and diversifies its supply chain, while boosting India's semiconductor ecosystem and reducing dependence on East Asian substrate suppliers. The factory will focus on producing glass-based substrates, which offer better thermal and mechanical properties than traditional organic substrates, enabling higher performance and density for advanced chips.

**Background**: Semiconductor substrates are the base materials on which chips are built, providing mechanical support and electrical connections. Most current substrates use organic materials, but glass substrates are emerging as a superior alternative for next-generation packaging. Intel's investment aligns with India's push to become a semiconductor manufacturing hub.
