## [Mercedes-Benz starts mass production of axial flux motors](https://media.mercedes-benz.com/en/article/bebac2af-acdc-465a-9538-adb0bf3d8ccf) ⭐️ 8.0/10

Mercedes-Benz has begun large-scale production of YASA's axial flux motors at its Berlin-Marienfelde plant, marking the first industrial-scale rollout of this compact and efficient motor design for electric vehicles. This milestone could significantly improve EV efficiency and reduce costs, as axial flux motors offer higher torque density and smaller size compared to traditional radial flux motors, potentially reshaping future EV powertrains. The axial flux motor, also known as a pancake motor, has its magnetic flux parallel to the rotation axis, enabling a flat, compact design. YASA's version uses a yokeless and segmented armature (YASA) topology to further reduce weight and improve performance.

hackernews · raffael_de · Jun 10, 07:44 · [Discussion](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=48472877)

**Background**: Most electric vehicles today use radial flux motors, where magnetic flux flows radially between rotor and stator. Axial flux motors, by contrast, have flux flowing axially, allowing a larger magnetic surface area and better cooling for a given volume, resulting in higher torque density. Mercedes-Benz acquired YASA in 2021 to bring this technology to mass production.

LLMs are increasingly used in financial services for customer support and transaction analysis, making them attractive targets for such attacks.

## [Google's DiffusionGemma Speeds Up Text Generation 4x](https://blog.google/innovation-and-ai/technology/developers-tools/diffusion-gemma-faster-text-generation/) ⭐️ 8.0/10

Google released DiffusionGemma, an experimental open model under Apache 2.0 license, that uses a diffusion-based approach to generate text 4 times faster than traditional autoregressive models. This breakthrough could dramatically improve user experience on edge devices like phones and local GPUs, where inference speed is critical, and may shift the AI industry toward more efficient generation methods. DiffusionGemma is a 26B Mixture of Experts (MoE) model that generates text in parallel rather than token-by-token, enabling faster inference with self-correction capabilities.

hackernews · meetpateltech · Jun 10, 16:09 · [Discussion](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=48478471)

**Background**: Traditional large language models (LLMs) generate text autoregressively, producing one token at a time, which is slow on edge devices. Diffusion models, originally used for image generation, can generate multiple tokens simultaneously by gradually denoising random noise into coherent text. This approach trades some accuracy for speed, making it ideal for real-time applications.
