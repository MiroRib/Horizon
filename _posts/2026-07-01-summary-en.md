## [FFmpeg 9.1 Introduces Improved Native AAC Encoder](https://hydrogenaudio.org/index.php/topic,129691.0.html) ⭐️ 8.0/10

FFmpeg 9.1 includes a new native AAC encoder that significantly improves audio quality, removing the experimental flag after seven years of development and addressing previous artifacts like chirping. This update matters because FFmpeg is a widely-used multimedia framework, and the improved encoder eliminates the need for users to install third-party AAC encoders like Apple's Core Audio, simplifying workflows and enhancing audio quality for millions of users. The encoder is transparent at 128 kbps for most samples, with artifacts only appearing in extreme cases, and it was mainly optimized for 48 kHz audio. The decoder also had a bug with stereo PNS (Perceptual Noise Substitution) that was worked around in the encoder.

hackernews · ledoge · Jul 1, 14:10 · [Discussion](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=48747116)

**Background**: AAC (Advanced Audio Coding) is a lossy audio compression format standardized by MPEG, designed to be the successor to MP3. FFmpeg's native AAC encoder had long been criticized for poor quality and artifacts, leading many users to rely on external encoders like libfdk_aac or Apple's Core Audio. The new encoder aims to close that quality gap.

## [IPFS Content Publishing 10x Faster via Async PUT RPCs](https://probelab.io/blog/optimistic-provide/) ⭐️ 7.0/10

ProbeLab introduced 'Optimistic Provide', a technique that makes most PUT RPCs asynchronous, reducing IPFS content publishing latency from over 13 seconds to under 1 second. This 10x speedup significantly improves user experience for IPFS content publishing, potentially boosting adoption in real-world applications where fast uploads are critical. The trade-off is that the operation is not exactly the same: control returns to the user after most (not all) PUT RPCs succeed, with remaining ones completing in the background.

hackernews · dennis-tra · Jul 1, 15:30 · [Discussion](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=48748518)

**Background**: IPFS uses a Distributed Hash Table (DHT) called Amino DHT to publish and locate content. Traditionally, content publishing required waiting for all PUT RPCs to complete, which could take tens of seconds in large, churning networks. Optimistic Provide reduces this by treating most PUTs as asynchronous, similar to optimistic concurrency control.
